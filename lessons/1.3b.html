1.3b — Dice Roller Warmup

Discovery Lab ~15 min

Now that you've cracked the random formula, apply it. Create a file called DiceTest.java.

Part 1: Three Dice, One Roll

  1. Roll three d6 dice independently — each one gets its own Math.random() call.
  2. Calculate and print the sum (like a 3d6 stat roll in your RPG).
  3. Print each individual roll AND the total.
Die 1: 4
Die 2: 6
Die 3: 2
Total: 12

Part 2: Critical Detection

  1. Roll a d20.
  2. Print whether it's a "critical hit" (rolled 20) or "critical miss" (rolled 1) or a normal roll (anything else).
  3. You'll need if statements. Look at the QuickRef if you haven't seen them before.
d20 roll: 20 — CRITICAL HIT!

Tinker: Push It Further

  1. Roll a d100 (1–100). Use it to simulate a loot drop: print "You found a legendary sword!" if the roll is 95 or above, "Rare item!" if 70–94, and "Common loot." for anything below.
  2. How many if blocks did you need? Is there a cleaner way to structure them?

Socratic Checkpoint

You wrote the random formula on the previous page. But in DiceTest.java, you're writing it three times — once for each die. What if your RPG needs to roll dice in 20 different places? How could you avoid copying and pasting the formula every time? (We'll solve this in 1.7.)

Checkpoint — before moving on, you must be able to:

  • Write the random formula correctly from memory — every time
  • Use if statements to check dice results
  • Print formatted dice output with labels
