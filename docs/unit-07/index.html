Unit 7: ArrayList

Arrays from Unit 6 are fixed-size. You decide the size when you create them, and that's it. But most collections in real programs grow and shrink. Your inventory shouldn't have a cap. Your spellbook shouldn't be locked at 8 slots. Your leaderboard shouldn't need a pre-determined size.

This unit introduces ArrayList — a dynamic, resizable list that grows as you add things. You'll also learn sorting (selection sort, insertion sort) to organize your collections, and the standard search pattern for finding items.

Concepts

TopicWhat you'll learn
ArrayList Basics Declaration, add, remove, get, set, size. Generic types.
Traversal & Algorithms For-each loop. Finding, counting, filtering, accumulating with ArrayList.
Sorting Selection sort and insertion sort. Comparing elements.

Your inventory from Unit 6 gets upgraded from Item[] to ArrayList<Item>. The sorting algorithms you learn here (selection sort, insertion sort) are the same ones that sort your inventory by value or your leaderboard by score. In Unit 8, 2D arrays work alongside ArrayList — the dungeon map is a grid, but your party roster is a dynamic list.
